Assumptions, inclusions & limitations

  • Federal tax uses 2024 IRS brackets with standard deduction ($14,600 for single filers), applied progressively — not averaged.
  • State tax uses flat effective rates per state from verified 2024 data embedded in this file.
  • Payroll taxes include Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) on gross salary.
  • Not accounted for: NYC/SF city income taxes, FICA wage base caps, Alternative Minimum Tax, OPT/F-1 visa FICA exemptions in the first years, investment gains tax.
  • Refinancing means replacing your existing student loan with a new private loan at a lower interest rate — typically possible after you have a job offer and income history. It can significantly reduce total interest paid.
  • Unemployment period — during this phase, loan interest compounds monthly and living costs (estimated at your per-year cost of attendance ÷ 12) continue.
  • Wealth projection — once your loan is fully repaid, the repayment amount shifts to an investment growing at 8% annual return (S&P 500 long-run average).
  • Salary progression is an approximation based on your input growth rate. Actual progression depends on company, role, and market conditions.
  • Experience bonus: 1–2 years adds ~4% to starting salary; 3+ years adds ~7%, reflecting stronger negotiation position.
